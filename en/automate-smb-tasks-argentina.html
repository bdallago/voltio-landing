How to automate manual tasks in an Argentine SMB

To automate manual tasks in an SMB, start with the repetitive, high-volume processes —data entry, emails, spreadsheets and frequent replies— by integrating the tools you already use. You don't need to replace all your systems: a diagnosis identifies what to automate first and how much time it frees up.

Why automate if "we've always done it by hand"?

In most Argentine SMBs, the owner or a handful of people concentrate tasks that repeat every day: copying data from one place to another, forwarding emails, updating a spreadsheet, answering the same WhatsApp questions. That time never shows up on a balance sheet, but it's time you're not spending on selling, serving customers, or growing.

Automating doesn't mean firing people or becoming a "tech company". It means the computer handles the boring, repetitive work, and people focus on what truly needs judgment.

+500 hours saved is what our automations added up to for clients. A daily 30-minute task is more than 120 hours per person per year.

Which tasks should you automate first?

Not everything at once. Start with what meets three conditions: it's repetitive, predictable, and frequent. Typical SMB examples:

  • Data entry between systems: moving info from an email, form or spreadsheet into your management system, without typing it by hand.
  • Follow-up emails and messages: reminders, confirmations and frequent replies you write one by one today.
  • Reports and spreadsheets: reports you assemble every week or month by copy-pasting.
  • Tool integration: making WhatsApp, Drive, Excel and your system "talk" instead of living apart.

Do I need to replace all my systems?

No. The most sensible —and cheapest— way to start is integrating what you already have. Most SMBs don't need to tear everything down: they need their current tools to work in sync. Replacing systems is only considered when the one you have really holds you back.
